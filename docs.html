Section 01
What is RealityCheck?
RealityCheck is a real-time truth network. Citizens post ground-level proof of what is happening at physical locations — traffic jams, crowded malls, long queues, full parking lots. The community validates these reports. Validated truth is stored permanently on the Polygon blockchain and cannot be deleted or edited by anyone, including the platform operators.

Core promise: "This report is on the blockchain. It cannot be deleted by anyone — not even us. The truth is immutable."

What problem does it solve?

You want to go to GVK One Mall on a Saturday afternoon. Is the parking full? Is the food court crowded? Google Maps shows you the location — it does not tell you the reality right now. RealityCheck does. The information comes from people who are physically there, verified by the community, stored permanently.

Section 02
How the System Works
The system has five layers. Each layer has one job. No layer does the job of another layer.
Layer 1 — Proof Layer
User captures reality. GPS location + timestamp + optional photo. This is the raw evidence before any community verification.
GPS · Timestamp · Photo (IPFS hash) · Category · State
Layer 2 — Blockchain Layer
Post, validations, and outcome stored on Polygon. Immutable. Cannot be deleted by anyone. This is the truth record.
PostCreated · ValidationSubmitted · PostFinalized · ReputationUpdated
Layer 3 — Validation Layer
Community confirms or disputes the post. Minimum 3 validators. 70% confirm threshold = VALID. Blind voting prevents herd bias.
3–5 validators · 70% threshold · Blind votes · Ground witness bonus
Layer 4 — Indexer Layer
Node.js service reads blockchain events, computes derived state, and serves the API. Rebuilds from chain if database is wiped.
Feed queries · Confidence scores · Staleness detection · Gap cards
Layer 5 — Credits Layer
Off-chain incentive system. Credits earned by contributing accurate posts and validations. Spent on platform features. No inflation by design.
Daily cap · Spending sinks · No transfer · No cash value

User Roles
There are four roles in the system. Three are user roles (earned through activity), one is an operational role (appointed by the platform). Roles are not exclusive — a Trusted User is also a Poster and a Validator.
📸
Poster
Any user who pays ₹5 to submit a reality update. No minimum reputation required. Everyone starts here.
✅
Validator
User who has made ≥1 valid post OR has reputation score ≥5.0. Can confirm or dispute others' posts. Cannot validate own posts.
🏆
Trusted User
Computed status, not a separate role. Score ≥20.0 (2,000 stored). Votes carry higher weight (3×). Shown as badge in UI. No contract change needed.

Independent contractor hired by the platform to seed activity in a new city. Paid milestone-based, not per post. Never posts themselves.

The Proof System
Every post has an initial proof score before community validation. Higher proof score = faster validation threshold reached = more credible post.

Proof score components

GPS accuracy20%Device GPS confirms location within 50m of claimed geohashPrimary. Cannot be faked without physical presence.
Freshness20%Post submitted within staleness window of last updateA 2-min-old report on a stale location scores high.
Photo evidence20%Photo uploaded, IPFS hash stored on-chainOptional but strongly encouraged. +20 points if present.
User reputation20%Poster's existing reputation score scaled to 0–20Expert poster's posts start with higher initial credibility.
Community validation20%Added after validation: (confirmCount/totalVotes) × 20Final proof score only known after finalization.
Initial Proof Score (before validation) ProofScore_initial = GPS(0–20) + Freshness(0–20) + Photo(0 or 20) + Reputation(0–20)

Final Proof Score (after validation) ProofScore_final = ProofScore_initial + Validation(0–20)

Maximum possible: 100 · Minimum for display: 0

Section 05
Validation Engine
Validation is the truth mechanism. Without it, a post is just a claim. The validation engine turns claims into verified truth.

Validation rules (all enforced in smart contract)

RuleValueWhy
Minimum validators3Prevents 1-person manipulation
Maximum validators5Prevents gas explosion, bounds loop
Confidence threshold70%Supermajority needed for VALID
Validation windowPer category (5–20 min)Matches reality change rate
Self-validationNot allowedCannot confirm own post
Rate limit10 validations/hourPrevents spam farming
Blind votingYes — votes hidden until window closesPrevents herd bias
EligibilityMust have ≥1 valid post OR score ≥5.0Cold start friendly

Outcome calculation

Confidence Score confidence = (confirmCount / totalVotes) × 100

Outcome Rules if totalVotes < MIN_VALIDATORS (3): outcome = INCONCLUSIVE
if confidence ≥ 70: outcome = VALID
if confidence < 70: outcome = INVALID

Example: 4 votes, 3 confirms → confidence = 75% → VALID ✓

Ground Witness Bonus

A validator who is physically within 500m of the reported location at validation time receives a "Ground Witness" bonus. Their vote carries 1.5× weight in the confidence calculation. This is determined by GPS proximity check at vote submission time.

Why blind voting matters: If validators can see prior votes, the first vote anchors all subsequent votes (herd bias). Studies show 40–60% of secondary voters simply follow the first vote. Blind voting forces each validator to make an independent decision based on the photo evidence alone.

Section 06
Reputation & Trust Score
Reputation is the system's memory. It tracks how reliable each user has been historically and uses that to weight their future contributions.

On-chain vs off-chain split

On-chain (stored in ReputationRegistryV1): Raw counts (totalPosts, validPosts, totalValidations, correctValidations) and current score integer. Permanent, immutable, cannot be manipulated.

Off-chain (computed in indexer): Display score (score ÷ 100), tier label, accuracy percentages, "people helped" metric, activity graphs.

Floor: score never goes below 1 if previously above 0

Section 07
Credits Economy
Credits are the platform's participation incentive. They have no cash value, cannot be transferred, and cannot be converted to money. Their sole purpose is to reward contribution and unlock platform features.

Anti-inflation design rule: Total credits that can be spent must exceed total credits that can be earned. Spending sinks are stronger than earning rates. This prevents credit hyperinflation which would make them worthless.

Credit inflation protection — the math

Maximum daily earn at cap Max earn = 10 credits/day

Example: active user, 30 days Total earn = 10 × 30 = 300 credits (theoretical maximum)

Minimum spend to maintain visibility Boost post (weekly) = 5 credits × 4 = 20 credits/month
Alerts (2 locations) = 2 credits/month

A very active user accumulates ~278 credits/month. Spending sinks create natural consumption. Casual users accumulate slowly.

Data flow

// User submits post User (UPI) → Razorpay → Relay Server → submitPost() → Polygon User (Wallet) → MetaMask → submitPost() → Polygon // Indexer reads events Polygon event → Indexer listener → PostgreSQL posts table ← PostCreated event validations table ← ValidationSubmitted event location_states ← PostFinalized (derived, updated) user_stats ← ReputationUpdated (derived, updated) // Frontend reads Frontend → REST API → PostgreSQL → User sees feed Frontend → REST API → /config → Zones, categories, active contract version // Credits (fully off-chain) PostFinalized event → Indexer → credits table → +2 credits to poster ValidationSubmitted + PostFinalized → credits table → +1 to validators

Multi-version contract support

When V2 contracts are deployed, the indexer reads events from both V1 and V2 addresses simultaneously. All data flows into the same database tables with a contract_version column for attribution. Frontend sees unified feed. No schema migration. No downtime.

Chain reorg safety

Polygon can reorg. Events from recent blocks may be replaced. The indexer waits for 10 block confirmations before writing derived state (location_states, user_stats). Chain mirror tables (posts, validations) are written immediately but marked confirmed=FALSE until 10 blocks pass. Practical effect: ~20 second delay between posting and appearing in feed.

Verified Active User (VAU) definition

A VAU is a user who has: (1) verified phone number via OTP, (2) made at least 1 post that reached VALID outcome, (3) submitted at least 5 validations, (4) had at least 1 correct validation. This definition cannot be gamed without physical presence near real locations.

Section 14
Frequently Asked Questions

For users

Why does posting cost ₹5?

The ₹5 fee prevents spam. Anyone can post anything for free — that produces noise, not truth. The fee creates accountability: if your post is invalid, you paid ₹5 for nothing. If it is valid, you earn credits and build reputation. Accurate posts are net positive. Spam posts are net negative.

What happens to my ₹5?

In the current model (Mode A), ₹5 goes to the platform's Razorpay merchant account as INR. It funds platform operations, relay gas costs, and CLP milestone payouts. In future Mode B (crypto enabled), the fee is split: 60% to poster reward pool, 20% to validator pool, 20% to platform.

My post was marked INVALID. Why?

Less than 70% of validators agreed with your assessment. Either the situation changed between your post and the validation window, or validators disagreed with your state label. INVALID posts reduce your reputation score by −1.0. Repeated invalid posts reduce validation weight over time.

Can I see who validated my post?

Not during the validation window — votes are hidden to prevent herd bias. After finalization, the validator addresses are visible on-chain at polygonscan.com by searching your post transaction.

What can I do with credits?

Credits boost your posts in the feed, enable location alerts ("notify me when GVK Mall is not busy"), unlock location analytics, and access the priority validation queue. Credits cannot be transferred, sold, or converted to money.

Is this like a gambling app?

No. Rewards are based on accuracy (a skill), not chance. The ₹5 is a quality fee, not a bet. Your outcome depends entirely on whether your reality report matches what the community observes — which is verifiable.

For validators

How do I become a validator?

Make one post that gets validated as VALID. That is the only requirement to start. Alternatively, reach a reputation score of 5.0 through any combination of accurate posts and correct validations.

What if I validate incorrectly?

Your reputation score drops by −0.10. This is intentionally light — wrong votes are not always obvious. Repeated wrong validations accumulate and eventually reduce your vote weight and eligibility.

Technical questions

Can the platform delete a post?

No. Posts are stored on the Polygon blockchain. Once a PostCreated event is emitted, it is permanent. Not even the smart contract owner can delete it. The platform can choose not to display a post in the feed, but the underlying data remains on-chain forever and is publicly readable by anyone.

What happens if the platform shuts down?

All post data, validation records, and reputation scores remain on the Polygon blockchain indefinitely. The indexer database can be rebuilt by anyone by replaying blockchain events from block 0. The frontend can be re-hosted. The smart contracts continue to operate — they have no off switch.

Is the contract audited?

Not yet. The contracts follow security best practices (CEI pattern, ReentrancyGuard, CEI, idempotency guards, replay protection) but have not been reviewed by an independent audit firm. An audit is planned before mainnet launch with significant volume.

What is the cryptoPaymentEnabled flag?

A one-way switch in the Core contract. Default is false — UPI only, no crypto on-chain, zero regulatory risk. When the owner calls enableCryptoPayment(), wallet users can pay MATIC directly and receive MATIC rewards. This cannot be reversed once enabled. UPI users continue to work in both modes.
